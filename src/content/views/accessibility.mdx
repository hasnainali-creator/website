# Accessibility

## Sport Belongs to Everyone. So Does Our Journalism.

---

Think about what sport means to you.

The rush of a last-minute winner. The tension of a final over. The silence before a penalty kick. The roar that follows a record-breaking performance.

Now imagine being unable to access the journalism that covers those moments — not because you lack interest, not because you lack intelligence, but simply because the platform delivering that journalism was not built with you in mind.

That is a failure. Not yours. The platform's.

At OmnySports, we refuse to accept that failure as inevitable. Sport is one of the most powerful unifying forces on this planet. It transcends geography, language, culture, and background. Our journalism exists to serve that universality — and that means serving every reader, regardless of ability, device, assistive technology, or circumstance.

This Accessibility page explains exactly where we stand, what we have built, what we are still building, and how you can help us do better.

As we have always maintained — and as deeply outlined in our [About Us](/about) page — OmnySports was built for every sports fan on Earth. This page is where that commitment becomes practical, measurable, and accountable.

## Our Accessibility Commitment

### A Promise, Not a Checkbox

Accessibility in digital media is frequently treated as a legal compliance exercise — something organisations address reluctantly, minimally, and only when required by regulation.

That is not how we approach it at OmnySports.

We approach accessibility the same way we approach our journalism: with genuine investment, continuous improvement, and a sincere belief that doing it properly matters. An inaccessible platform is not just a legal liability. It is an editorial failure. A story that cannot be read by a visually impaired fan using a screen reader is a story that has failed part of its audience. A video that plays without captions is commentary that excludes a deaf supporter who cares just as deeply about the sport as anyone else.

**Our accessibility commitment is simple:**

Every reader who comes to OmnySports deserves the same quality of experience — regardless of how they navigate the web, what assistive technology they use, or what physical, cognitive, or sensory challenges they may face.

We are not there yet. We will tell you exactly where we are. But we are committed to getting there — and we are moving toward that goal with urgency and genuine intent.

## Our Current Accessibility Standard

### WCAG 2.1 — The Global Framework We Work Toward

OmnySports is actively working toward full compliance with the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines — WCAG 2.1 — at Level AA.**

WCAG 2.1 is the internationally recognised standard for web accessibility, developed by the World Wide Web Consortium. It organises accessibility requirements into four foundational principles — a website must be Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust. These four principles are commonly referred to as the **POUR framework.**

Here is what each principle means in practice for OmnySports:

---

### Perceivable

Information and content on our platform must be presented in ways that every user can perceive — regardless of sensory ability.

**What this means for us:**

* All images carry descriptive alternative text so that screen reader users receive a meaningful description of every visual element
* Video content is accompanied by captions and, where resources allow, audio descriptions
* Content is never conveyed through colour alone — visual information is always supplemented by text or other indicators
* Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
* Background and foreground colour combinations meet minimum contrast ratio requirements

---

### Operable

Every function and navigation element on our platform must be operable by every user — including those who cannot use a standard mouse or touchscreen.

**What this means for us:**

* All platform functionality is accessible via keyboard navigation alone
* No content on our platform requires interaction that a user cannot perform with a keyboard or switch device
* Navigation is consistent and predictable across all page types
* Users are never timed out of a session without adequate warning and the ability to extend
* No content flashes more than three times per second — protecting users who are susceptible to photosensitive seizures

---

### Understandable

Content and navigation must be understandable to every user — including those with cognitive disabilities or those using translation tools.

**What this means for us:**

* All pages are written in clear, plain English — the same commitment to clarity that governs our journalism as detailed in our [Editorial Policy](/editorial-policy)
* The language of every page is correctly declared in the HTML so that screen readers and translation tools can process it accurately
* Error messages on forms and interactive elements are clear, specific, and helpful — not vague or technical
* Navigation is consistent across the platform so users can always predict where they are and where they are going
* Instructions never rely solely on sensory characteristics such as shape, size, or position

---

### Robust

Our platform must be built on code that is robust enough to be reliably interpreted by a wide range of assistive technologies — now and as those technologies evolve.

**What this means for us:**

* Our HTML is written to current web standards with proper semantic structure
* Interactive elements use appropriate ARIA labels and roles where native HTML semantics are insufficient
* Our platform is tested regularly against major assistive technologies — including leading screen readers across both desktop and mobile environments
* We monitor our codebase for accessibility regressions every time new features or platform updates are deployed

## What We Have Already Implemented

We believe in transparency — which means telling you not just where we are going, but where we currently are.

The following accessibility features are already active across OmnySports:

**Structural and Navigation Accessibility**

* Logical heading hierarchy across all page types — H1 through H4 — allowing screen reader users to navigate content structure efficiently
* Skip navigation links at the top of every page, enabling keyboard users to bypass repeated navigation menus and reach main content directly
* Consistent, predictable navigation structure across all sections of the platform
* Breadcrumb navigation on article and category pages for clear location orientation

**Visual Accessibility**

* Alternative text applied to all editorial images and graphics across our content archive
* Colour contrast ratios meeting WCAG 2.1 AA minimum requirements across core page elements
* Text resizing supported without loss of functionality up to 200%
* No content conveyed through colour alone

**Content Accessibility**

* Plain English writing standard applied across all content types — from breaking news to long-form analysis
* Clear labelling of content types — reporting, analysis, and opinion are always distinguished, as described in our [Disclaimer](/disclaimer)
* Sponsored and commercial content is always clearly labelled, never presented in a format that could confuse any reader — including those with cognitive disabilities

**Technical Accessibility**

* Semantic HTML structure across all core page templates
* Keyboard navigability across all primary platform functions
* ARIA landmark roles implemented on core page regions
* Form elements correctly labelled across all user-facing input areas including our [Contact Us](/contact) page

## Our Accessibility Roadmap

### What We Are Still Building

Honest accessibility reporting means acknowledging where gaps remain. We have identified the following areas as current priorities in our ongoing accessibility improvement programme:

**Video and Multimedia Content** We are in the process of implementing comprehensive closed captions across all video content on our platform. Auto-generated captions are currently active on newer video content. Human-reviewed captions — which are significantly more accurate — are being progressively applied to our content library. Audio descriptions for visual-only content are in our development roadmap for the next platform release cycle.

**Third-Party Embedded Content** Some content on OmnySports includes embeds from third-party platforms — social media posts, data widgets, and video players. The accessibility of embedded third-party content is ultimately within the control of those platforms, not OmnySports. We are actively working to replace or supplement third-party embeds with natively accessible alternatives where technically feasible.

**Complex Data Visualisations** Our analytics and statistics content includes complex data visualisations — charts, performance maps, and comparative graphics. Making complex visual data fully accessible to screen reader users is one of the most technically challenging areas of web accessibility. We are developing text-based data summaries to accompany all complex visualisations, ensuring that the analytical insight contained in a graphic is always available in a fully accessible format.

**Mobile Accessibility** Our platform is mobile-first by design. We are conducting ongoing accessibility audits specifically focused on the mobile experience — including touch target sizing, gesture-based navigation alternatives, and screen reader performance on iOS and Android environments.

**Cognitive Accessibility** Beyond technical compliance, we are actively working to improve the cognitive accessibility of our content and platform design. This includes reducing unnecessary complexity in navigation, improving the clarity of error messages, and ensuring that our reading experience is as low-stress and intuitively navigable as possible for users with cognitive or attention-related disabilities.

## Assistive Technology Compatibility

OmnySports is designed and tested for compatibility with the following categories of assistive technology:

**Screen Readers** Our platform is developed with compatibility for leading screen reader software across desktop and mobile environments. We test against major screen reader and browser combinations regularly as part of our development cycle.

**Keyboard Navigation** Full keyboard navigation is supported across all primary platform functions. Users who rely on keyboard-only navigation — including those using switch access devices — can access all core content and functionality without requiring a mouse or touchscreen.

**Voice Control** Our platform supports voice control navigation software. Interactive elements are correctly labelled to ensure voice command users can activate links, buttons, and form fields by speaking their visible labels.

**Browser Zoom and Text Resizing** Content on OmnySports remains fully functional and readable when browser zoom is applied up to 200%. Text can be resized without content overlapping, truncating, or becoming inaccessible.

**High Contrast and Dark Mode** Our platform respects operating system-level high contrast and dark mode settings, ensuring that users who rely on these display modes for visual comfort or necessity receive a consistent and functional experience.

## Accessibility and Our Editorial Standards

Accessibility is not separate from our editorial values. It is an extension of them.

The same commitment to clarity that drives our [Editorial Policy](/editorial-policy) — the insistence on plain language, logical structure, and content that respects the reader's intelligence — directly serves our accessibility goals. Clear writing is accessible writing. Logical page structure is accessible page structure. Content that is easy to understand for a general audience is content that is more likely to work well for users with cognitive disabilities.

The work of our [Editorial Team](/editorial-team) is therefore part of our accessibility programme — not just our legal compliance team. Every journalist who writes clearly, every editor who structures an article logically, every sub-editor who writes a descriptive image caption is contributing to an accessible platform.

Accessibility and quality journalism are not competing priorities at OmnySports. They are the same priority, approached from different angles.

## How to Tell Us About an Accessibility Issue

### Your Feedback Is One of Our Most Valuable Tools

No accessibility audit — however thorough — catches everything. Real users, using real assistive technologies in real environments, encounter issues that testing environments never replicate.

If you encounter an accessibility barrier on OmnySports — on any page, in any content type, on any device — we want to know about it immediately. Your report directly informs our development priorities.

**When you contact us, please include:**

1. The URL of the specific page where you encountered the issue
2. A description of what you were trying to do
3. A description of what happened instead
4. The assistive technology and browser you were using, if known
5. Any additional context that would help us reproduce and understand the issue

Reach us through our [Contact Us](/contact) page and mark your message clearly as an **"Accessibility Report."** Every report is reviewed by our development and editorial team. We acknowledge all accessibility reports within 48 hours and provide a substantive response — including our assessment of the issue and our timeline for addressing it — within five business days.

We do not treat accessibility reports as complaints to be managed. We treat them as contributions to a better platform — and we are genuinely grateful for every one we receive.

OmnySports uses a small number of third-party services and embeds in connection with our platform — including analytics tools, advertising networks, and social media embeds. We will investigate whether a native accessible alternative can be provided and pursue the matter with the relevant third-party provider where appropriate.
