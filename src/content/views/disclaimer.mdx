# Disclaimer

## Read This Before You Act on Anything You Read Here.

---

Sport moves fast. News breaks in seconds. Opinions shift with every match. Data updates in real time.

In that environment, context matters enormously. Knowing *what* you are reading is just as important as *what it says*. A match prediction is not a guaranteed outcome. A transfer report is not a confirmed signing. An analyst's opinion is not the official position of OmnySports as an organisation.

This Disclaimer page exists to make those distinctions absolutely clear.

It is not legal small print designed to protect us from accountability. It is an honest, transparent explanation of the nature and limitations of the content we publish — written with the same clarity and respect for our readers that defines everything we do at OmnySports.

As we have always maintained — and as deeply outlined in our [About Us](/about) page — OmnySports was built because sports fans deserve honesty. That honesty begins here, on this page, before you read a single article.

---

## General Disclaimer

### The Nature of Our Content

OmnySports is a sports journalism and analytics platform. Every piece of content we publish — match reports, transfer news, performance analysis, opinion columns, statistical breakdowns, and editorial commentary — is produced for **informational and journalistic purposes only.**

While we invest heavily in accuracy, verification, and editorial rigour — as comprehensively governed by our [Editorial Policy](/editorial-policy) — the nature of live sports coverage means that information can and does evolve rapidly. Events develop. Official positions change. Data is updated. Stories that were accurate at the time of publication may require updates as new information emerges.

**OmnySports content should never be used as the sole basis for:**

* Financial decisions of any kind
* Sports betting or wagering activity
* Fantasy sports selections in high-stakes competitions
* Professional, legal, or medical decisions
* Any action where the consequences of acting on inaccurate information could cause harm

We are a news and analysis organisation. We are not financial advisors, legal counsel, medical professionals, or licensed betting consultants. Our content should be consumed as informed journalism — not as professional advice of any kind.

## Content Accuracy Disclaimer

### We Strive for Accuracy. We Cannot Guarantee Perfection.

OmnySports maintains rigorous editorial standards. Our verification process, our source requirements, our fact-checking procedures, and our correction protocols are all defined in detail in our [Editorial Policy](/editorial-policy) — and they are applied to every piece of content we publish, without exception.

However, we operate in a fast-moving, live information environment. The following realities apply to content on our platform:

**Breaking News** During developing stories, confirmed and unconfirmed information exists simultaneously. We clearly label developing stories and distinguish between verified facts and unconfirmed reports. However, the situation may change between the moment we publish and the moment you read.

**Live Match Data** Real-time statistics, scores, and match data are sourced from third-party data providers. While these providers maintain high accuracy standards, occasional discrepancies can occur in live data feeds. OmnySports is not responsible for temporary inaccuracies in live data that have not yet been identified and corrected by our editorial team.

**Historical Data** Statistical records and historical data are sourced from established sports databases. While we verify data carefully, records are occasionally revised by official governing bodies after initial publication. We update our content when we become aware of such revisions.

**Third-Party Quotes and Statements** Where we report statements made by athletes, managers, officials, or organisations, we attribute them accurately to the source. OmnySports is not responsible for the accuracy of statements made by third parties and reported in good faith.

When errors occur — and in any organisation producing content at scale, they inevitably will — we correct them promptly, transparently, and without concealment. Our correction standards are among the most rigorous in digital sports media.

## Opinion and Analysis Disclaimer

### Know What You Are Reading

One of the most important things we can do for our readers is make absolutely clear when they are reading a statement of fact and when they are reading a statement of opinion or analysis.

At OmnySports, all content falls into one of three clearly distinguished categories:

**Reporting** Factual, verified journalism based on confirmed information from credible sources. This content represents what we know to be true at the time of publication, supported by evidence.

**Analysis** Interpretation of verified facts, data, and events by our editorial and analytics team. Analysis is evidence-based and rigorously reasoned — but it represents the informed judgement of the author, not a statement of guaranteed fact.

**Opinion** Clearly labelled perspective pieces representing the personal viewpoint of the named author. Opinion content on OmnySports does not represent the institutional position of OmnySports as an organisation unless explicitly stated.

Every content type is clearly labelled on our platform. If you are reading an opinion column, it is labelled as such. If you are reading an analytical piece, it is presented as analysis. If you are reading a news report, it is governed by our full verification standards.

The work of our [Editorial Team](/editorial-team) is guided by the principle that readers deserve to know not just *what* they are reading, but *what kind of thing* they are reading. This distinction is non-negotiable in our newsroom.

## Predictions and Forecasting Disclaimer

### Sport Is Unpredictable. Our Analysis Is Not a Guarantee.

OmnySports publishes match previews, performance projections, tactical forecasts, and data-driven predictions across all sports we cover. This content is produced by experienced analysts using verified historical data, current form metrics, and contextual intelligence.

It is also, by its very nature, speculative.

Sport is defined by its unpredictability. The entire reason billions of people watch it is because no outcome is guaranteed. The underdog wins. The favourite collapses. The player projected for a historic performance gets injured in the warm-up.

**Our predictions and forecasts:**

* Are produced using rigorous analytical methodology
* Are based on verified data and informed expertise
* Are clearly labelled as analytical projections — not guaranteed outcomes
* Should never be used as the basis for betting, wagering, or financial decisions

OmnySports accepts no liability for any outcome — financial or otherwise — resulting from decisions made based on our predictive or analytical content. If you choose to use our analysis as one input among many in your own decision-making, that is entirely your right. If you treat it as a guarantee, you are misusing it — and this disclaimer makes that clear.
