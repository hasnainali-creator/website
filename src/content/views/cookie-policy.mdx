## What Exactly Is a Cookie?

### Understanding the Technology Before the Policy

Before we explain how OmnySports uses cookies, it is worth understanding what a cookie actually is — because most platforms never bother to explain it.

A cookie is a small text file. That is it. When you visit a website, that website can instruct your browser to save a tiny file on your device — your computer, your phone, your tablet. That file contains a small amount of data, usually a unique identifier or a record of a preference or action.

The next time you visit that website, your browser reads the file and sends the information back to the website's server. This allows the website to recognize you, remember your preferences, keep you logged in, or understand how you are using the platform.

**A simple example:** You visit OmnySports and adjust your display preferences — perhaps you prefer dark mode. A cookie saves that preference. The next time you visit, the site reads the cookie and loads dark mode automatically. Without the cookie, you would need to set that preference every single time.

Cookies cannot access other files on your device. They cannot execute programmes. They cannot carry viruses. A cookie is simply a text file — a small memory that a website stores on your browser to improve or measure your experience.

## Types of Cookies — A Clear Breakdown

Not all cookies are the same. Understanding the different categories helps you make informed decisions about which ones you are comfortable with.

### Session Cookies vs Persistent Cookies

**Session Cookies** exist only for the duration of your visit. The moment you close your browser, they are deleted automatically. They serve purely functional purposes — keeping you logged in as you navigate between pages, for example.

**Persistent Cookies** remain on your device after your browser is closed, for a defined period of time. They are used for purposes like remembering your preferences on your next visit or measuring how often you return to the platform.

---

### First-Party Cookies vs Third-Party Cookies

**First-Party Cookies** are set directly by OmnySports. They are used exclusively by us and can only be read by our platform.

**Third-Party Cookies** are set by external services that operate on our platform — such as analytics tools or advertising networks. These cookies are governed by the privacy policies of those third-party providers, not solely by OmnySports.

We keep our use of third-party cookies to an absolute minimum. Where third-party cookies are active on our platform, they are identified clearly in the cookie categories below.

## The Cookies OmnySports Uses

### Category One — Strictly Necessary Cookies

**These cookies cannot be switched off. They are required for our platform to function.**

Strictly necessary cookies do not collect any information about you for marketing or analytics purposes. They exist purely to make the technical operation of our website possible.

**What they do on OmnySports:**

* Maintain your login session if you have a registered account
* Remember your cookie consent preferences so we do not ask you every time you visit
* Enable secure page-to-page navigation without losing your session
* Support basic security functions that protect the platform and its users
* Process form submissions correctly — including through our [Contact Us](/contact) page

**Duration:** Most strictly necessary cookies are session cookies and are deleted when you close your browser. Consent preference cookies persist for up to 12 months so your choices are remembered.

**Can you disable them?** These cookies cannot be disabled through our consent panel because disabling them would prevent the platform from functioning. You can block them through your browser settings, but doing so will significantly impair your experience on OmnySports.

---

### Category Two — Performance and Analytics Cookies

**These cookies help us understand how our platform is being used — so we can make it better.**

Performance cookies collect anonymised, aggregated information about how visitors interact with OmnySports. They tell us which articles are most read, which pages load slowly, where users navigate to after reading a piece, and how long people spend engaging with different content types.

This information is used exclusively to improve our platform. It is never used to build individual user profiles for advertising purposes.

**What they measure on OmnySports:**

* Which pages and articles receive the most traffic
* How users navigate through the platform during a session
* Page load speeds and technical performance metrics
* Which devices, browsers, and screen sizes our readers use most
* Geographic distribution of our readership — at country and regional level, not individual address level

**Are these first-party or third-party?** We use industry-standard analytics tools to process performance data. These tools set third-party analytics cookies on our platform. All data processed through these tools is anonymized before analysis — individual users are never identified.

**Duration:** Analytics cookies typically persist for between 30 days and 26 months, depending on the specific measurement purpose.

**Can you disable them?** Yes. You can disable performance and analytics cookies through our consent management panel at any time. Disabling them does not affect your ability to use our platform — it simply means we receive less data about how the platform is performing.

---

### Category Three — Functional Cookies

**These cookies remember your preferences and personalize your experience.**

Functional cookies are not strictly necessary for our platform to work — but they make your experience significantly better. They remember choices you have made so you do not have to repeat them every time you visit.

**What they remember on OmnySports:**

* Your preferred display settings — such as text size or theme preferences
* Your preferred sports or content categories — so relevant content is easier to find
* Whether you have dismissed certain notifications or prompts — so we do not show you the same message repeatedly
* Your language or regional settings if applicable

**Duration:** Functional cookies typically persist for between 90 days and 12 months.

**Can you disable them?** Yes. Disabling functional cookies means that personalization features will not work and your preferences will not be remembered between visits. Your ability to access all content on OmnySports is not affected.

---

### Category Four — Advertising and Targeting Cookies

**These cookies are connected to advertising content served on our platform.**

OmnySports carries digital advertising to sustain our journalism. As explained in full in our [Advertising Policy](/advertising-policy), our commercial relationships are managed through a strict editorial firewall — advertising revenue never influences our editorial content.

In connection with advertising delivery, certain advertising cookies may be active on our platform. It is important to understand what these cookies do — and what they do not do on OmnySports.

**What advertising cookies do on OmnySports:**

* Enable contextual advertising — serving ads related to the general topic of the page you are reading
* Measure basic advertising performance — how many times an ad was displayed and whether it was clicked
* Prevent the same advertisement from being shown to you an excessive number of times

**What advertising cookies do NOT do on OmnySports:**

* Build detailed individual profiles based on your browsing history across the wider internet
* Share your personal data with advertisers — we do not sell your data under any circumstances, as detailed in our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy)
* Track your activity across third-party websites outside of OmnySports

**Duration:** Advertising cookies typically persist for between 30 and 90 days depending on the specific campaign parameters.

**Can you disable them?** Yes. You can disable advertising cookies through our consent management panel. Disabling them means you will still see advertisements on OmnySports — but those advertisements will not be contextually matched to the content you are reading. The quantity of ads you see is not affected by your cookie preferences.

## How We Obtain Your Consent

### Your Choice Is Always Respected

The first time you visit OmnySports, a cookie consent panel is displayed clearly on your screen. This panel:

* Explains the categories of cookies we use in plain language
* Allows you to accept all cookies, decline all non-essential cookies, or customize your preferences by category
* Does not use pre-ticked boxes — no category is activated without your active choice, except strictly necessary cookies which are required for the platform to function
* Does not use dark patterns — confusing designs intended to nudge you toward accepting more cookies than you intend to

Your consent preferences are saved and respected on every subsequent visit. We do not ask you again unless your preferences have expired or you choose to update them.

## How to Change or Withdraw Your Consent

### You Are Always in Control

Consent given is not consent locked in forever. You can review, update, or withdraw your cookie preferences at any time through the following methods:

**Through Our Consent Panel** Access our cookie preference centre at any time through the cookie settings link in our platform footer. From there you can adjust your preferences by category and save your updated choices instantly.

**Through Your Browser Settings** Every major browser provides built-in tools to view, manage, and delete cookies stored on your device. The specific steps vary by browser — consult your browser's help documentation for exact instructions. Please note that managing cookies through browser settings affects all websites, not just OmnySports.

**Through Third-Party Opt-Out Tools** For third-party analytics and advertising cookies, you can also use industry opt-out tools such as the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on and the IAB Europe Transparency and Consent Framework tools where applicable.

Withdrawing consent for non-essential cookies does not affect the lawfulness of any processing that took place before you withdrew consent.
